Yeah, **0.0271 with lambda=0 is the smoking gun** — the BitOPs penalty isn't even active, yet it's still stuck at minimum bit-width. That means the controller is defaulting to 2-bit on its own, which means **Bug 1 is your real problem**, not lambda at all.

The batch-averaging in `select_bits` is causing the Gumbel-Softmax to consistently sample 2-bit because averaging logits across the batch before sampling collapses the distribution. The controller never gets a proper gradient signal to learn anything meaningful.

You need to fix Bug 1 first before lambda matters at all. Quick fix in `DQ_resnet.py`:

```python
def select_bits(logits, bit_options, temperature=1.0, training=True):
    bit_tensor = torch.tensor(bit_options, dtype=torch.float, device=logits.device)
    if training:
        soft = F.gumbel_softmax(logits, tau=temperature, hard=True)  # (B, num_bits)
        return (soft * bit_tensor).sum(dim=-1).mean()  # mean over batch for scalar loss
    return bit_tensor[torch.argmax(logits, dim=-1)].float().mean()
```

The `.mean()` at the end keeps it scalar for your loss computation without destroying the per-instance gradient. Then after that's working and you're seeing AvgBitOPs around 0.016+ (4-bit range) with lambda=0, *then* reintroduce lambda at 0.0001.
